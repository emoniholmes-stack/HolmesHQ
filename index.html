Ethics Check #1

Being made in the Image of God means that I have been created with the ability to love and create and reason. That understanding shapes the way I view AI as a tool. AI is something that humans created, and it can do a lot of things like: generate, analyze, and sound humanlike, but it doesn't have the unique creative thoughts that the Lord allows our minds to create. Because of that we should see AI as a resource not a replacement. It can help us, again, generate, analyze, organize, and brainstorm ideas, but should not and cannot replace our own critical thinking skills. Knowing that we reflect the Image of God should remind us that we should have good discernment when using AI, or just technology in general.

One boundary that I keep when using AI is that I will never let it write or create something that is meant to represent my own voice. For example, I can use AI to create an outline for business papers, but should never replace the thoughts and critical thinking put into this. This makes sure that what I produce stays authentic to my voice and values. AI should be a support system, not a substitute. God gave us the ability to create and think and we should never undermine that or take advantage of it because of new technology.

Something that helps me use AI with humility is taking time to reflect and pray before and after using it. When using AI, we should remind ourselves that we are responsible for what we produce, but ultimately having the wisdom to use it well comes from God. After using AI, we should review what it suggested and make sure we aren't copying directly from what it generated. We should again use discernment, and apply our principles and values. This will help keep me aware that AI is a tool that we steward because the Lord allows us to use it.

Ethics Check #2

This term, one of the main things I’ll be working on is creating a website. I plan to use AI to help me with ideas, coding support, and design suggestions. AI can be really useful when I need quick answers or creative input, but it’s not always accurate. Sometimes it gives me code that doesn’t actually work when I test it, or it suggests designs that look good in theory but don’t fit my layout. There are also times when AI will confidently explain something that’s just plain wrong, especially when it comes to newer coding updates. Because of that, I can’t just take everything it says at face value.

When I use AI for building my website, I make sure to verify everything. If AI gives me a piece of code, I’ll test it myself and see if it runs correctly. If it doesn’t, I'll go back and ask it what factor I added that breaks the website and ask it to fix it. When it comes to content, like writing a short bio or explaining what my site is about, I’ll check that the information is true and that it actually represents me.

A biblical principle that connects with this idea of being truthful is Proverbs 12:22, which says, “The Lord detests lying lips, but he delights in people who are trustworthy.” This verse reminds me that honesty isn’t just about what we say, it’s about how we work. If I’m building something like a website, I want it to reflect truth, not just creativity or skill. Using AI responsibly means being careful with what I post and making sure it’s accurate and authentic. In the end, the goal isn’t just to make something that looks good, it’s to build something that’s honest, reliable, and honors God in the process.

Ethics Check #3

In business, I often work with private information. For example, when I handle financial records, customer accounts, or billing statements, I might see names, payment details, or other sensitive info. It’s important to handle that carefully so people can trust me.

I will never share personal or financial details like social security numbers, bank account info, or anything confidential. If I ever need to use an example or show something for work or learning, I will get permission first. I will make sure the person knows what I need, why I need it, and how it will be used. That way, I respect their privacy and stay professional.

A scripture that guides me is Philippians 2:4, which says, “Let each of you look not only to your own interests, but also to the interests of others.” Respecting someone’s private information is one way to do that in business. It shows care for them, protects them, and keeps my work honest. Handling sensitive info the right way is not just about following rules, it is about loving people and honoring God in everything I do.

Ethics Check #4

For my site, I plan to respect ownership by only using content I created or content I have permission to use. If I use someone else’s work, I’ll credit them clearly and follow the licensing rules they set. A Creative Commons license I’d use is CC BY because it lets others use my work as long as they give proper credit, which keeps it open but respectful. The Bible tells us in Romans 13:7a to “Give to everyone what you owe them,” and that includes honoring people for their ideas and creativity.

Serving with a Pure Heart

“Serve wholeheartedly, as if you were serving the Lord, not people.” — Ephesians 6:7 (NIV)

Serving God isn’t just about doing good things. It’s about having the right heart behind what we do. Anyone can help others, but when we serve out of love for God, it changes everything. It’s not about getting attention or feeling important. It’s about honoring Him through our actions and showing His love wherever we go.

There are times when you will serve and you could start to feel tired or unnoticed. Maybe you’re helping someone and they don’t say thank you, or you give your time, and no one seems to care. It’s easy to wonder if it really matters. But God sees what people don’t. He notices the quiet moments, the sacrifices, and the effort that others might overlook. He values a heart that serves for Him and not for recognition.

Serving with a pure heart also means keeping a good attitude. Sometimes we’re called to help when it’s not convenient or when people are hard to love. That’s where God grows us. When we serve even when it’s tough, he blesses us in that. When our hearts stay focused on Him, our service becomes more than something we have to do, it becomes worship.

Here are some questions you can think about:
What’s one way you can check your heart before serving others?
How can you keep your focus on God when your efforts go unnoticed?

Next Step: Find one person you can serve this week with no expectation of anything in return. Do it quietly, from the heart, and take a moment afterward to thank God for letting you be His hands and feet.
